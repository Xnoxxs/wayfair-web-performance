# **Wayfair Performance Audit Report** 

**Website Name:** Wayfair 

**Website URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/</u> 

## **Why I Chose This Website** 

I chose Wayfair because it is a large e-commerce website for home furniture with a wide variety of content and features, making it a strong candidate for a performance audit. The website contains static pages, dynamic product listings, search functionality, filters, user authentication, large image galleries, and API-driven content. These different types of pages and interactions provide many opportunities to identify performance issues and recommend meaningful improvements. 

Another reason I selected Wayfair is that its homepage already receives a **red Performance score (31)** on Google's PageSpeed Insights (mobile), indicating that there are clear optimization opportunities to investigate throughout the project.

## **Pages Included in the Audit** 

### **1. Homepage** 

**URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/</u> 

The homepage is the first page many users visit and creates the first impression of the website. It includes promotional banners, featured products, navigation menus and many images, making it an excellent starting point for evaluating overall performance. 

### **2. Living Room Furniture Category** 

**URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/furniture/cat/living-room-furniture-c45974.html</u> 

This category page displays many products simultaneously while providing filtering, sorting, and pagination features. It is useful for analyzing how large product lists, images, and interactive filters affect loading performance. 

### **3. Search Results** 

**URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/keyword.php?keyword=sofa</u> 

Search pages rely heavily on dynamic content and user interaction. They perform multiple network requests while loading products and updating filters, making them valuable for evaluating JavaScript execution and data fetching performance. 

### **4. Product Page** 

**URL:** _<u>https://www.wayfair.com/furniture/pdp/17-stories-6-drawer-dresser-with-clothes-rack-and-chargi ng-station-w113487844.html?piid=715440082</u>_ 

Product pages are among the most important pages on any e-commerce website. They include image galleries, customer reviews, product information, recommendations, pricing, and interactive components, making them ideal for identifying rendering and loading performance issues. 

### **5. Shopping Cart** 

**URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/v/checkout/basket/show</u> 

The shopping cart is a critical part of the purchasing process. Good performance here directly affects the user experience and can influence whether customers complete their purchases. 

### **6. Ideas & Advice** 

**URL:** <u>https://www.wayfair.com/sca/ideas-and-advice</u> 

Unlike the product-focused pages, this section contains editorial content, inspiration articles, and rich media. Including it provides a broader view of the website by allowing comparisons between content-heavy pages and shopping pages.
